# Industrifonden — Investment Thesis

## Thesis Summary

Industrifonden backs Nordic breakthrough innovations — unique, scalable technologies and science with potential to create lasting societal impact. Its evergreen structure (no fixed fund life, no LPs demanding distributions on a clock) underwrites long-horizon bets in deep tech and life science where time-to-revenue is measured in years, not quarters. Patient, science-led capital with public-mission DNA, deployed via three sector practices: Deep Tech, Life Science, and Transformative Tech.

## Stage Rationale

Primary entry at seed and Series A. The evergreen structure means initial checks can be modest (10M SEK) but follow-on capacity is "considerably more" — so they can ride a winner from first institutional round through to growth without ownership-defending pressure of a traditional fund cycle. This is structurally well-suited to deep-tech and biotech timelines.

## Sector Rationale

Three explicit practice areas, each with a dedicated Practice Lead:
- **Deep Tech** (Patrik Sobocki) — advanced materials, industry 4.0, energy, advanced computing, AI, synthetic biology
- **Life Science** (Jonas Jendi) — biotech, medtech, healthtech addressing unmet medical needs
- **Transformative Tech** (Per Anell) — B2B business models creating new industries

Pattern: capital-intensive, IP-heavy, science-led businesses where Swedish/Nordic research base produces global leaders (Oatly, Arcam, CellaVision, Bonesupport pattern).

## What They Look For

- Breakthrough technology or science with defensible IP
- Nordic founding teams or R&D base
- Markets with global scalability, not just Nordic
- Long-horizon plays where evergreen patience is a feature, not a bug
- Solutions to "major global challenges" (climate, health, industrial decarbonization)

## What They Avoid

- Short-cycle consumer plays without scientific moat
- Pure software with no deep-tech / life-science angle (Transformative Tech mandate is B2B with structural change, not generic SaaS)
- Geographies outside the Nordics (Swedish public-mission roots constrain mandate)

## Follow-On Policy

Evergreen structure → no fixed reserves model. They explicitly state capacity to invest "considerably more" than the 10–50M SEK first ticket. Implication: they reserve heavily and lead or co-lead follow-on rounds where conviction is high.
