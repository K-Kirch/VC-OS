# STOAF (Stockholm Business Angels) — Investment Thesis

## Thesis Summary

Aggregate Stockholm angel capital and domain expertise into a structured "competence house" that backs Swedish early-stage companies "creating new industries and making a difference in the world." Three vertical lanes — Deep Tech, Sustainable Energy Tech, Life Sciences — each led by a domain-experienced GP, supported by an investment committee and a network of ~50 certified angels. Distinguishing posture: combines investment with formal angel education and certification.

## Stage Rationale

Seed / early-stage. The angel-collective format is best suited to small, early checks where domain GP plus angel-network value-add can be decisive — and where institutional VCs are typically not yet active.

## Sector Rationale

Three verticals reflect the GP team's domain expertise:

- **Deep Tech**: led by Magnus Eriksson (ex-Gartner Consulting Group VP, Vattenfall nuclear physics). Bet: complex technology businesses requiring patient capital and technical understanding.
- **Sustainable Energy Tech**: led by Klaus Gottwald (CEO Stoaf Management; serial entrepreneur). Bet: energy transition creates new industrial categories.
- **Life Sciences**: led by Claes Post (30+ years pharma; ex-HealthCap, Nordic Biotech, Danske Life Science, Karolinska Innovation, Almi Invest). Bet: pharma/medtech require scientific operator at the cap table.

## What They Look For

- Swedish early-stage companies in deep tech, sustainable energy, or life sciences
- Technology with potential to "create new industries"
- Founders open to angel-syndicate involvement and active board engagement
- Proximity to Stockholm ecosystem (most angels Stockholm-based)

## What They Avoid

- Sectors outside the three verticals
- Stages beyond early (institutional Series A+)
- Geography outside Sweden / Stockholm region
