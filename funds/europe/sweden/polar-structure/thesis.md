# Polar Structure — Investment Thesis

## Thesis Summary

Be the responsible long-term transition partner for sustainable infrastructure. Combine operational infrastructure expertise with evergreen capital to (a) co-found and majority-own new infrastructure companies in green transition and digital backbone, and (b) take minority positions in deep-tech that accelerates infrastructure deployment. The thesis is that the green transition requires patient, knowledgeable capital that can sit between PE return horizons and pure VC volatility.

## Stage Rationale

Two distinct stage profiles:
1. **Founder/majority-owner** of greenfield infrastructure businesses — not stage-bound; acts as build-from-zero capital.
2. **Minority co-investor** in growth-stage deep-tech with infrastructure adjacency (e.g., Einride). Typically post-Series A, capital-intensive technology bets.

## Sector Rationale

Three platforms reflect a coherent green-transition thesis:

- **Sustainable Transport**: train depots, railway yards, ports, logistics, transport hubs — answer to declining state investment in rail/port maintenance
- **Green Energy**: charging infrastructure, energy storage, renewables, district heating/cooling — answer to grid bottlenecks blocking electrification
- **Digital Solutions**: LoRaWAN networks, fiber, 5G, real-estate connectivity — answer to digital backbone gaps

## What They Look For

- Sustainable-transition infrastructure with public/private partnership angle
- Deep-tech companies whose deployment depends on or accelerates infrastructure rollout
- Long-duration cash flows
- Operational complexity where infrastructure know-how creates value
- Partners willing to give Polar Structure majority control (own-build) or significant minority (co-invest)

## What They Avoid

- Pure software / non-infrastructure venture
- Short-duration consumer plays
- Sectors outside the three transition platforms
