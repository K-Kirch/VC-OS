# Inventure — Investment Thesis

## Thesis Summary

Inventure backs "the few" — a small number of exceptionally ambitious founding teams per year in the Nordics and Baltics, with full commitment. Their thesis is that transformative companies are built by rare individuals with the conviction to pursue what others consider impossible. By backing fewer companies more deeply, Inventure can offer genuine partnership rather than portfolio management. They are generalists by design, believing great founders emerge across all sectors.

## Stage Rationale

Pre-seed and seed because Inventure wants to be the first institutional believer. They have been the first institutional investor in 87% of their portfolio. This early entry is central to their identity — they find founders before the opportunity is obvious and commit before others will.

## Sector Rationale

Deliberately generalist — Wolt (marketplace), Swappie (circular economy), Algorithmiq (quantum computing), Lassie (insurtech), ReOrbit (space tech). They follow founder quality and problem conviction, not sector heat. The Nordics and Baltics geography defines their mandate, not any specific vertical.

## What They Look For

- Raw ambition — founders who want to make something that improves life for many people
- Willingness to operate under deep uncertainty — "we're at our best when things look really uncertain"
- Evidence of grit and willingness to pivot when reality demands it (Wolt pivoted, Swappie pivoted)
- Nordic or Baltic roots (born, educated, or building their company in the region)
- A big unsolved question at the heart of the company — not a polished pitch deck

## What They Avoid

- Founders optimising for the funding round rather than the mission
- Companies outside the Nordic/Baltic geography (mandate is explicit)
- Incremental ideas with no potential to accelerate many people's lives

## Follow-On Policy

Inventure reserves capital for follow-on and actively supports portfolio companies in raising subsequent rounds from larger Nordic and international funds.
