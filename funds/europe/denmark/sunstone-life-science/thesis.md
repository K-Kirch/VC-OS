# Sunstone Life Science Ventures — Investment Thesis

## Thesis Summary

Sunstone Life Science Ventures backs science-driven biotech and medtech companies emerging from the strong Nordic research base. Their thesis is that the Nordics produce world-class life science research — particularly from Danish, Swedish, and Finnish universities and hospitals — but the commercialisation infrastructure historically lagged behind the US and UK. Sunstone fills this gap by combining capital with deep sector expertise and strong connections to pharma and medtech industry partners.

## Stage Rationale

Life science companies have inherently long development timelines and require capital partners who understand the clinical and regulatory path. Sunstone focuses on early to mid-stage because this is where life science-specific expertise creates the most value — designing clinical programmes, securing regulatory strategy, and building scientific advisory boards.

## Sector Rationale

The Nordics have a disproportionate concentration of life science expertise relative to population — driven by public investment in research infrastructure, universal healthcare systems that generate high-quality clinical data, and a tradition of collaboration between academia and industry. Sunstone is positioned to capture this deal flow better than generalist VCs.

## What They Look For

- Strong scientific foundation — typically spin-outs from leading Nordic universities or hospitals
- A differentiated mechanism of action or platform with broad applicability
- Clinical proof of concept or a credible path to Phase II data
- Management teams with prior biotech or pharma experience (or willingness to bring it in)
- Assets in areas of high unmet medical need
- Potential for partnership with large pharma or medtech companies

## What They Avoid

- Me-too compounds without differentiated clinical profiles
- Companies where the founding team lacks life science commercialisation experience and won't supplement it
- Assets in areas already saturated with well-capitalised competitors
