# 2150 — Investment Thesis

## Thesis Summary

2150 invests exclusively in technology companies solving the defining challenge of our time: making cities sustainable, liveable, and resilient. Their thesis is that urbanisation is irreversible — over two-thirds of the global population will live in cities by 2050 — and the systems that run those cities (energy, buildings, mobility, food, water, waste) are profoundly broken. The companies rebuilding these systems represent one of the largest investment opportunities in history.

## Stage Rationale

2150 invests from seed through Series B because urban tech companies often require longer development cycles and deeper integration with physical systems than pure software companies. Early entry allows them to shape company strategy and be a long-term partner through the inherently longer journey.

## Sector Rationale

Urban systems are responsible for approximately 75% of global CO2 emissions. Unlike horizontal climate tech funds, 2150 goes deep on the urban lens — this includes built environment, mobility, energy infrastructure, food systems, water, and waste, but always through the lens of what happens at city scale.

## What They Look For

- Technology-first companies — software, hardware + software, or data platforms (not project developers or consultancies)
- A clear path to city-scale or national-scale deployment
- Founders with systems thinking — understanding how their solution integrates with existing urban infrastructure
- Strong scientific or engineering foundation
- Measurable environmental or social impact that is core to the business model, not a marketing layer
- European companies with global ambition

## What They Avoid

- Pure carbon offset or credit businesses
- Hardware-only companies without a software or data layer
- Companies where the climate angle is secondary to the business model
- Single-city solutions without a replicable model

## Follow-On Policy

2150 has reserved significant capital for follow-on. They expect to lead or participate in Series B and C for top portfolio companies and bring in international co-investors at those stages.
