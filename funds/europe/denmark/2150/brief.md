## Fit by Use Case

### Syndicating a deal
**Fit:** High (if climate tech, urban tech, or built environment — Seed to Series B)
**Rationale:** Active lead investor with a clear mandate; brings domain expertise and strategic co-investors (infrastructure funds, city operators) that generalist VCs cannot.
**Right contact:** Verify at 2150.vc/team — likely Christian Hernandez or sector-relevant partner

### Lining up the next round
**Fit:** High (for Series A → B in climate/urban tech)
**Rationale:** 2150 reserves significant follow-on capital and aims to lead or participate at Series B. They also connect portfolio to international climate investors at later stages.
**Right contact:** Lead GP for the portfolio company's sector

### Competing against them
**They lead with:** Sector focus and credibility (generalists can't match their climate depth), urban tech network (cities, infrastructure partners)
**Their weakness:** Mandate is narrow — if a company has even marginal climate relevance they will claim it; check for thesis stretch. Younger fund with no exits yet.
**How to differentiate:** If you have a stronger international growth platform or the company is not genuinely urban/climate tech
