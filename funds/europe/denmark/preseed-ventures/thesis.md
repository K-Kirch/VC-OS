# PreSeed Ventures — Investment Thesis

## Thesis Summary

PreSeed Ventures backs Danish founders at the very earliest stages — typically before product-market fit is established — with small initial tickets and high conviction. Their thesis is that Danish entrepreneurs are systematically underserved at the pre-seed stage, and that early-stage capital combined with hands-on mentorship produces better outcomes than waiting for companies to be more "fundable." They are willing to take on more uncertainty than most VCs in exchange for earlier entry.

## Stage Rationale

Pre-seed and seed because this is where Danish founders face the most acute capital gap. Angel and family-and-friends rounds can take companies only so far; PreSeed fills the gap before international seed funds engage.

## Sector Rationale

Broad within technology — they do not maintain a strict sector focus, reflecting the reality that great Danish founders emerge across many domains. They tend to avoid sectors requiring very large initial capex.

## What They Look For

- Danish founders with a genuine, specific problem they are solving
- Early evidence of customer interest — even informal validation counts
- Technical or domain credibility — founders who have earned the right to build this
- Capital efficiency — companies that can make significant progress on small cheques
- Coachability — founders who actively seek and incorporate feedback

## What They Avoid

- Founders who cannot clearly articulate the problem they are solving
- Ideas requiring significant regulatory approval before any revenue can be generated
- Markets so niche that venture-scale returns are structurally impossible

## Operational Style

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| **Decision Speed** | Fast — often 2–4 weeks |
| **Board Involvement** | Active at board level; also informal mentorship |

## Follow-On Policy

PreSeed will often lead or participate in seed rounds for companies that progress well. They are explicit about helping portfolio companies raise their next round from larger Danish and international funds.
