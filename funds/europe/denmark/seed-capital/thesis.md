# Seed Capital — Investment Thesis

## Thesis Summary

Seed Capital backs ambitious Nordic technology founders at seed and Series A with a focus on companies building scalable software, marketplace, and consumer products. They prioritise exceptional founding teams with deep domain knowledge and look for early evidence of product-market fit before committing capital. With over two decades of Danish VC history, they offer founders access to a strong network of portfolio founders and experienced operators across the Nordics.

## Stage Rationale

Seed Capital focuses on seed and early Series A because they believe the most transformative value-add from a VC happens in the first 12–24 months of a company's life — shaping go-to-market, hiring the first key team members, and avoiding structural mistakes that are hard to unwind later.

## Sector Rationale

Their sector focus reflects the strengths of the Nordic ecosystem: strong engineering talent, high software adoption rates, and a culture of trust-based business relationships that produces strong B2B SaaS founders. Consumer and marketplace investments typically reflect founders with genuine consumer insight from operating in a digital-first society.

## What They Look For

- Founders with unfair insight into the problem — domain expertise or lived experience
- Evidence of early product-market fit, even if at small scale
- Large addressable markets with a clear wedge strategy
- Teams with complementary skills (technical + commercial co-founders preferred)
- Nordic or Scandinavian market as the beachhead, with credible international expansion logic
- Capital efficiency mindset — founders who can do more with less

## What They Avoid

- Pure idea-stage pitches with no product or customer validation
- Founders who cannot articulate why now is the right time for their company
- Markets dominated by entrenched incumbents with no structural wedge
- Companies requiring very large upfront capex before generating revenue

## Follow-On Policy

Seed Capital reserves capital for follow-on and will typically participate in Series A rounds for portfolio companies showing strong growth. They do not lead follow-on rounds externally but will defend their pro-rata.
