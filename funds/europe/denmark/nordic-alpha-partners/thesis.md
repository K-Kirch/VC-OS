# Nordic Alpha Partners — Investment Thesis

## Thesis Summary

Nordic Alpha Partners focuses on growth-stage Nordic technology companies that have achieved product-market fit and are ready to scale internationally. Their thesis is that Nordic companies are disproportionately good at building efficient, capital-light software businesses but often lack the growth capital and international network needed to break out of the region. NAP provides both.

## Stage Rationale

Series A through growth because they believe their value-add — internationalisation support, executive network, and growth capital — is most relevant once a company has a proven model. Earlier stage requires a different type of support that generalist growth funds are less equipped to provide.

## Sector Rationale

Technology broadly, with a preference for B2B SaaS and marketplace models that are capital-efficient and have clear international portability. Nordic companies in these categories have strong unit economics and tend to punch above their weight internationally.

## What They Look For

- Proven product-market fit with paying customers
- €1M+ ARR or equivalent traction metric
- Strong retention — net revenue retention above 100% for SaaS; repeat purchase for marketplace
- A credible international expansion plan, not just ambition
- Management teams with or willing to hire international commercial talent

## What They Avoid

- Pre-revenue or pre-product-market-fit companies
- Companies reliant on a single customer or geography
- Businesses with structurally poor unit economics that growth won't fix
