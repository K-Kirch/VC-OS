# EIFO — Investment Thesis

## Thesis Summary

EIFO is not a commercial venture fund — it is the Danish state's market-correcting investment instrument. The thesis is simple: private capital markets underfund certain types of Danish innovation (deep tech, life sciences, early stage) due to information asymmetry and risk tolerance constraints. EIFO co-invests alongside private VCs to correct this market failure and stimulate the broader Danish startup ecosystem, without crowding out private capital.

## Stage Rationale

EIFO operates across all stages because market failures occur at different points depending on sector. Deep tech and life sciences face the biggest gaps at seed and Series A; growth-stage Danish companies can face gaps in later rounds if lacking international VC backing.

## Sector Rationale

Sector-agnostic by mandate — EIFO invests across all sectors as long as the company is Danish (headquartered or substantially operating in Denmark). However, they have specific programmes for green transition, life sciences, and deep tech reflecting government policy priorities.

## What They Look For

- Danish company (HQ or primary operations in Denmark)
- A credible private VC lead investor (EIFO does not lead rounds)
- Scalable business model with potential for international growth
- Evidence of innovation — not a copy-paste of existing solutions
- Alignment with Danish or EU policy priorities (bonus, not requirement)

## What They Avoid

- Companies without a private co-investor (EIFO follows, never leads)
- Purely Danish market businesses without international scale potential
- Companies in sectors with sufficient private capital availability (EIFO applies a market failure test)

## Operational Style

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| **Decision Speed** | Slower than commercial VCs — government processes apply |
| **Board Involvement** | Observer rights only in most cases; not active board members |

## Follow-On Policy

EIFO will typically follow-on in subsequent rounds for portfolio companies if the private lead investor continues. They pro-rata their position but do not lead.
