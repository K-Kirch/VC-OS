## TL;DR

Denmark's state investment fund — co-invests alongside private VCs into Danish companies. Not a commercial investor; moves slower and requires more documentation than private funds. Useful for Danish deals where the company needs additional capital and the round has a private lead. Does not replace a commercial lead. Appeared in most major Danish rounds including Pleo, Vivino.

---

## Fit by Use Case

### Syndicating a deal
**Fit:** Medium (Danish companies only; you must already be leading)
**Rationale:** EIFO follows, never leads. If you are leading a Danish round and the company wants additional capital, EIFO can fill part of the round. Process is slower and more paperwork-intensive than a commercial co-investor.
**Right contact:** Apply via eifo.dk programme; identify the relevant Investment Director for the company's sector

### Lining up the next round
**Fit:** Low as primary target — they follow, not lead
**Rationale:** EIFO can fill a slot in a round but cannot lead it. Only relevant after you have secured a private lead for your portfolio company.
**Right contact:** eifo.dk programme application

## Portfolio Conflicts

EIFO co-invests broadly across the Danish ecosystem — portfolio conflicts are unlikely to be an issue given their government mandate, but check for any programme restrictions if relevant.

---

## Outreach

**Best entry point:** Programme application at eifo.dk; identify the right programme for the company's stage and sector
**Cold email:** Not applicable — use formal channel
**Response speed reputation:** Slower than commercial VCs; expect 4–8 weeks from application to decision
