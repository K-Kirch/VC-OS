# Northzone — Investment Thesis

## Thesis Summary

Northzone backs founders with high conviction from seed through growth, across Europe and the US. Their thesis is built on long-term partnership — showing up with conviction and staying through the hard times, not just the good ones. They position themselves as the antidote to playbook-driven VC: rather than generic advice, they provide access to a curated network of 500+ operators who have solved the exact problem a founder is facing right now.

## Stage Rationale

Seed through growth because Northzone believes their value compounds over time with the same founder. Starting at seed allows them to shape company direction early; their growth platform and large fund size means they can support companies all the way to IPO without forcing founders to find new lead investors at each stage.

## Sector Rationale

Broad technology — consumer, enterprise, fintech, health, climate — with concentration in categories where European founders have historically excelled. Their portfolio reflects the full breadth of the Nordic and European ecosystem, which has produced global category leaders in multiple verticals.

## What They Look For

- Founders who can sustain conviction through adversity — "against the odds" is their positioning
- Long-term thinking over short-term optimisation
- Companies with the potential to define a category, not just compete in one
- Founding teams that are coachable but not follower types — they want strong opinions, loosely held
- European or Nordic founders with genuinely global ambitions

## What They Avoid

- Founders looking for passive capital
- Companies without a path to category leadership

## Follow-On Policy

Northzone has large reserves and actively leads follow-on rounds for top portfolio companies. Their fund size ($3.1bn+ AUM, latest fund $1.2bn) gives them significant follow-on capacity. They have supported companies from seed to IPO multiple times (Spotify, Klarna, Trustpilot, Kahoot).
