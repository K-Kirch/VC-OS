# Playbook — Competitive Intelligence

**When to use:** You're in a competitive deal process and need to understand the other funds in the room — what they offer, how to differentiate, and how to read the situation.

---

## Step 1 — Fast brief on the competing fund (brief.md)

Read `brief.md` immediately:

1. **TL;DR** — Understand who they are in 3 lines
2. **Compete on** — What do they lead with? (brand, speed, operator network, check size, geography)
3. **Their weakness** — What founders say they lack
4. **How to differentiate** — Pre-built talking points

This should take under 2 minutes.

---

## Step 2 — Understand their decision process (thesis.md)

Read `thesis.md`. In a competitive process, their process is your lever:

- **Decision speed** — If they're slow and you're fast, move fast and say so explicitly
- **Board involvement** — If they're heavy board operators and the founder wants space, highlight your approach
- **What they look for** — Know what they're evaluating so you can anticipate what they'll say in the room
- **What they avoid** — If the company has something on that list, they may pass anyway (saving you the fight)
- **Follow-on policy** — Founders care about who'll be there for the next round. If their follow-on is weak, that matters.

---

## Step 3 — Use their portfolio as a signal (portfolio.md)

Read `portfolio.md`. Look for:

- **Pattern matches** — Companies like yours they've backed = they understand the space but also = possible conflict
- **Anti-portfolio** — Companies like yours they've passed on (if known) — signals they may not believe in the model
- **Exit track record** — If you're talking to a later-stage founder, their exits matter more than their logo
- **Notable companies** — What they lead with in conversations with founders; you'll face these comparisons

---

## Step 4 — Assess their relationship with the founder (relationships.md)

Read `relationships.md`:

- Have they co-invested with this founder before? Hard to beat a prior relationship.
- Do you have a warmer relationship than they do? Use it — time with the founder before the formal process matters.
- Any portfolio founders who know this competing fund well? Ask them what the experience is like — real data beats positioning.

---

## Step 5 — Know their team (team.md)

Read `team.md`. Understand:

- Which partner would lead this deal for them?
- What's their style and reputation with founders?
- Do any of your team members have a strong relationship with that partner? (Could inform whether a collegial conversation vs. hard competition is better)

---

## Common failure modes

- Talking down the competing fund to the founder — always backfires
- Competing on dimensions the founder doesn't care about (read the founder, not the fund)
- Assuming you know the competing fund without checking — funds evolve, mandates change
- Ignoring that the founder may already have a preference and just be running a process
