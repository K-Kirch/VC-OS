# Playbook — Lining Up the Next Round for a Portfolio Company

**When to use:** A portfolio company is approaching a fundraise and you're helping them build a target investor list or get warm intros.

---

## Step 1 — Screen for stage and sector fit (brief.md)

Read `brief.md` first:

1. **Deployment status** — Active only. No point in pursuing a fund raising or between funds.
2. **Fit: Next round** — Pre-assessed fit rating. Use this as a first filter.
3. **Portfolio conflicts** — Critical. If they've backed a direct competitor, they cannot invest and the founder should not pitch them (risks IP leakage). Mark as excluded immediately.

---

## Step 2 — Confirm investment parameters (profile.md)

Read `profile.md`. Verify against the company's raise:

- **Stage focus** — Does their stage match where the company is now?
- **Check size** — Does their typical check fit the round size being raised?
- **Sector focus** — Exact match required. A fund that lists "Fintech" but mostly does payments won't be excited about insurance.
- **Ownership target** — If they need 20% and the round won't give them that, they'll pass or reprice.
- **Geography focus** — Some European funds won't lead outside their home country.

---

## Step 3 — Check their portfolio for social proof (portfolio.md)

Read `portfolio.md`. Look for:

- Companies at a similar stage that went on to raise successfully — signals they know how to support this journey
- Any direct or adjacent competitors (Conflict? column) — exclude if yes
- Portfolio companies the founder can reference or get intros from — this is gold

---

## Step 4 — Understand what they look for (thesis.md)

Read `thesis.md`. Before any intro:

- Map `what_they_look_for` against the company — is there a genuine fit or are you forcing it?
- Check `what_they_avoid` — any hard blockers?
- Note `decision_speed` — set founder expectations accordingly
- Note `board_involvement` — some founders don't want an active board member; some funds won't invest without a board seat

---

## Step 5 — Identify the right partner and path in (team.md → relationships.md)

Read `team.md` to find the partner who covers this sector/stage.
Read `relationships.md` to find the fastest path to that person:

- Existing co-investment relationship = strongest possible intro (reach out directly)
- Warm intro path via portfolio founder = second best
- Your firm's direct relationship = third
- Cold outreach = last resort, only if intro_preference allows it

---

## What to prepare for the founder

- Suggested intro email (short, specific, referencing the right partner and their thesis)
- Context on the fund: what they care about, who the right partner is, typical timeline
- Portfolio conflict flag if any — make sure the founder knows before they start talking to anyone

---

## Common failure modes

- Missing a portfolio conflict (always check the Conflict? column — don't rely on memory)
- Intro to the wrong partner (wasted social capital)
- Introducing a company at the wrong stage (too early / too late for the fund's mandate)
- Not flagging decision speed to the founder (mismatched timelines blow up rounds)
